DataFest — CourseKata Engagement Analysis

January - May 2024

Award: Winner of the Best Insight Award at DataFest

Overview#

Analyzed 1.5 GB of student engagement data from CourseKata to uncover what drives meaningful learning interactions. Delivered actionable insights that helped stakeholders understand how interactivity influences engagement and learning outcomes.

Key Contributions#

  • Tools & Methods: Python for data cleaning, processing, and visualization; merged session logs with content metadata to assess engagement quality.

Findings

  • Strong positive correlation (r ≈ 0.727) between interactive content and time-on-page.
  • Pages with coding exercises and interactive widgets drove ~70% increase in time-on-page compared to static pages.
  • Higher engagement on interactive pages was associated with increased student confidence, confirming the time spent was high-quality learning.

Impact

  • Recommended highlighting interactive “activation” features to boost retention. Insights were presented to stakeholders and earned recognition for Best Insight.

Reflection#

This project taught me how interactivity shapes user behavior. By combining engagement metrics with content type, I was able to identify features that genuinely enhance learning outcomes. I’m excited to bring this data-driven approach to product teams, helping them pinpoint which features drive long-term retention.

Notes#

Data are proprietary to CourseKata and the American Statistical Association’s DataFest; cannot be shared publicly.
